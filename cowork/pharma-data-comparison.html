Pharma Patient Data Regulations — Country Comparison

Can Takeda store identifiable patient data directly, or is a Trusted Third Party (TTP) required? Seven markets compared.

Headline verdict: No country outright prohibits pharma from holding identifiable patient data — and all seven countries require pharma to hold it for pharmacovigilance.
The "pharma can't hold patient data" narrative is mostly a myth, strongest in France for a specific data flow. TTPs are industry risk management, not universal law.

Key takeaway for Takeda: Architecture designed for France's requirements (TTP for HCP-referred enrolments + HDS-certified hosting) will largely satisfy all other markets in scope. Germany requires the most robust consent design. Japan requires cultural sensitivity beyond what the law alone demands. The pharmacovigilance exception is universal — all seven regulators legally require pharma to hold identifiable patient safety data, which is the single strongest counter-argument to the "pharma can't hold patient data" narrative.
